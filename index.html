How QTM works in practice

QTM runs entirely on‑chain. There is no centralized custody and no opaque order book. The contract itself holds a large liquidity pool of tokens and sells directly to buyers using MATIC/POL, with a minimum price curve anchored to Chainlink's MATIC/USD oracle.

  1. Connect a wallet to the Polygon network and ensure you hold MATIC/POL for gas and purchases.
  2. Visit the official PolygonScan page and interact with the verified contract, or use a dApp that integrates QTM.
  3. The contract reads the latest Chainlink MATIC/USD price and enforces a rising minimum USD price per whole token.
  4. When you buy, QTM moves directly from the contract pool to your wallet – no middleman, no custody risk.

This mechanism gradually increases the minimum on‑chain price as demand consumes supply, reflecting QTM's ambition to become a high‑value, scarce digital asset over time.

Security stack

Security is not a marketing slogan for QTM – it is the core design principle. The contract is built on modern OpenZeppelin components and extends them with additional verification layers:

  • Role‑based access control separates admin, minting, audit and emergency powers, reducing single‑point‑of‑failure risk.
  • Pausable operations allow transfers and mints to be frozen in extreme scenarios, similar to circuit breakers in traditional markets.
  • Reentrancy protection shields purchase and withdrawal flows from common smart‑contract attack patterns.
  • Combined ECDSA + Lamport signatures on quantum‑related actions, blending today's battle‑tested crypto with post‑quantum‑resilient schemes.
  • On‑chain security metrics track complexity, verification levels and audit checkpoints to reward more secure behavior over time.

The result is a token that behaves more like hardened infrastructure than a short‑lived speculation chip.
