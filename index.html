Vision

Security Architecture

QTM is built using OpenZeppelin Contracts v5 on Polygon, with several layers of protection designed for institutional‑grade security:

    >Role‑based access control (AccessControl) for granular admin, minting and emergency roles. >Pausable transfers and mints so that critical operations can be frozen in emergencies. >ReentrancyGuard to protect purchase and withdrawal flows from reentrancy attacks. >ECDSA + Lamport signatures on quantum‑related operations, combining classical and post‑quantum resistance. >Chainlink price feeds to anchor the token’s minimum price in USD with reliable oracle data. >On‑chain security metrics tracking complexity, verification levels and audit checkpoints.

How QTM public sale works

At deployment, 100 million QTM were minted to the founder reserve wallet and 900 million QTM were minted to the token contract itself. The contract sells QTM directly using MATIC/POL through a dynamic pricing curve based on Chainlink MATIC/USD data.

    >Connect a wallet to the Polygon network with MATIC/POL balance. >Use the official PolygonScan page (Write Contract → de>buyTokens) or any dApp that integrates QTM to buy from the contract. >The contract checks the latest Chainlink price and enforces a rising minimum USD price per token with every purchase. >Purchased QTM are transferred from the contract’s 900 M pool to the buyer’s wallet.
